about

Mateusz Cendlak – historical horns player. He studied both the modern and historical horn at Ignacy Jan Paderewski Music Academy in Poznań, Poland and at the Royal Conservatory in The Hague, the Netherlands. He took part in masterclasses conducted by Teunis van der Zwart, Erwin Wieringa, Christian Binde, Krzysztof Stencel and Jacek Muzyk.

On a daily basis Mateusz Cendlak is a member of the Musicae Antique Collegium Varsoviense, a period insturments orchestra of the Warsaw Chamber Opera. Besides, he played with or still collaborates with such orchestras as: the Orchestra of the Eighteenth Century, Capella Cracoviensis,, Haydn Philharmonie Eisenstadt, Bremer Barockorchester, Minsk Chabmer Soloists, Wroclaw Baroque Orchestra, {oh!} Orkiestra Historyczna, Royal Baroque Ensemble.

Mateusz led the class of historical horns at the 15th Early Music Forum at Frederick Chopin Music University in Warsaw and at the 24th and 25th International Summer Academy of Early Music in Warsaw.
